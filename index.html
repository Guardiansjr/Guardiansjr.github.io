The History of the Rubik's Cube

The Rubik's Cube was first invented in the 1980's by Erno Rubik, a math teacher who enjoyed giving his students fun and challenging puzzles. At that time, Erno Rubik named it the 'Magic Cube'. It took him about a month to finally be able to solve it. After seeing the potential of the "Magic Cube", he agreed to let Toy Corp mass produce it.

The Fastest Cubers

If you asked anyone cuber in the world who the fastest cuber is, they would probably say Feliks Zemdegs. Although Feliks Zemdegs doesnt hold the world record for the fastest 3x3 time, he does hold the world record for the 3x3 average. Meaning in 5 solves, the average of the 5 solves is faster than anyone else in the world. The average was 6.54 seconds. He solves the cube in a different way you would find on the internet if you search up how to solve a Rubik's Cube. The method that he uses is called CFOP. Most fast cubers in the world use this method too. The current world record holder for the fastest 3x3 time is held by Collin Burns with a time of 5.25 seconds. But Feliks Zemdegs has come close to this time with a time of 5.39 seconds.
